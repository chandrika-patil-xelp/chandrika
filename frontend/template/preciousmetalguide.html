Precious Metals Guide
Metals have been documented to be used by humans as early as 6000BC, ancient Egyptians and Mesopotamians were the earliest of civilizations to make and use jewellery out of metals. Gold and Platinum carry the highest demand and value amongst all metals due to their noble properties and rarity.
Gold
Gold has been the most sought after metal for jewellery making due to its rarity and lustre. Its value was quickly realised by humans and was used a mode of payment from as early as 3100BC.
Purity
Gold found in nature is in its purest form or also referred as 24 karat gold, pure gold is too soft to make jewellery and hence as a common practice is mixed with other metallic elements such as copper and zinc to form an alloy that is considerably stronger without sacrificing too much of its lustre. The popular Karat weights and their pure gold percentages are as listed below:
24 karat: 100% gold or Pure gold
22 karat: 91.7 % gold
18 karat: 75.0 % gold
14 karat: 58.3 % gold
12 karat: 50.0 % gold
10 karat: 41.7 % gold
9 Karat: 37.5% gold
Due to physical properties of a diamond and other gemstones, they can only be set in 18 Karat or lower purity of gold as anything above 18Karats will be too soft to hold on to the stones.
IP Address
Gold though is naturally yellow can be given another clour by mixing it with different alloy metals. The most popular colours of gold jewellery are
Yellow Gold: An alloy made up of Pure gold + Copper + Zinc
White gold: An alloy made up of Pure Gold + a white metal like Palladiaum, nickel or silver
Rose Gold: An alloy made up of Pure Gold + Copper
An important point to note is that the colour of the gold jewellery doest effect the value of the jewellery, it is the purity that dictates the value.

Purity
Platinum though naturally tough and hard needs to be mixed with certain metals to manufacture jewellery, for a piece of jewelry to be labeled as "platinum" it must have a minimum level of purity of at least 95% pure platinum. A purity level of less than 95% would require the metal be identified as a Platinum alloy
