The Kimberly Process
At Jzeva we are 100% committed to ensuring that all our diamonds are sourced ethically with no harm caused to people, society or environment. We ensure that our sourcing happens under strict guidelines as defined in the Kimberley Process.
The Kimberley Process (KP) is a joint government, industry and civil society initiative put together to control the rough diamond trade across the world and ensure that the trade doesn’t help finance rebel’s movements against legitimate governments.
All diamonds used in Jzeva jewellery are 100% conflict free, hence ensuring that we help you make a responsible purchase.

Carat
Carat is the unit of weight used to measure the mass of a diamond. A diamond of 1 Carat is exactly 200 Milligrams (0.2 grams). All else being equal, the price per carat increases with carat weight, since larger diamonds are both rarer and more desirable for use as gemstones.
It is worth noting that the price of a diamond does not increase on a linear scale with increases in the carat weight of a diamond. There are various weights above which there is a steep increase in value. For example, 2 diamonds of 1 Carat each might together cost you less than a single diamond of 2 Carats.
Clarity
As everything else in this word, diamonds are not perfect, Diamonds have internal features called inclusions and blemishes. These internal features effect the grading of a diamond giving it a clarity rating ranging from Flawless to Included.
Only 20% of the diamonds mined across the world are fit to be used in jewellery the rest are just relegated to industrial use. Having said that, a flawless diamond is so rare that it is possible to spend a lifetime being part of the jewellery industry but never see one. Flawless diamonds command the highest prices.

Cut
Diamond cutting is an art of processing a rough diamond obtained from a mine to making it into a gemstone usable in jewellery. The precision in which a diamond is cut decides how much light it reflects. Cuts are graded as follows:
Excellent Cut to the most exacting standards these diamonds will reflect the maximum possible light. The hearts and arrows patterns formed will be the clearest in case of Excellent cut diamonds.
IDEAL Exquisite quality cut to create the optimal combination of brilliance and fire. Reflects nearly all light that enters the diamond.
VERY GOOD Superior quality cut that reflects nearly as much light as the ideal cut while at a substantially lower cost.
GOOD Premium quality cut to optimize the size without hampering quality or beauty. Reflects most light that enters.
FAIR Adequate quality cut which reflects some light while maximizing weight. While not as brilliant as a good cut, still a quality diamond. Jzeva doesn’t use Fair cut diamonds in its designs.
POOR Inadequate quality cut that reflects minimal amount of light. Brilliant Earth does not provide diamonds with cut grades of poor. Jzeva doesn’t use Poor cut diamonds in its designs.
