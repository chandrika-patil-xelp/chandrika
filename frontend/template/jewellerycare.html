Jewellery Care
Jewellery is an important and a well thought of buy, knowing how to care for it can make all the difference in maintaining its original splendour over time and keep it sparking for generations to come.
Caring for
your jewellery
Do not expose precious metals to chemical, as doing so may lead to discolouration.
Remove your jewellery while performing labour intensive tasks like cleaning, cooking, gardening, lifting heavy items etc.
Put on jewellery after applying makeup as cosmetics and hairsprays may contain chemicals that can harm the metals.
Wearing jewellery while swimming n swimming pools is big no, as the chlorinated water might have damaging effects on the precious metals.
Do not undertake any sport or workouts while wearing jewellery as it might harm other and end up damaging your jewellery as well.
Ensure that you do not bring your jewellery in contact with other jewellery or metals as the ensuing friction might damage it.
Always keep your jewellery in the original Jzeva case provided along with your jewellery. When not in the box or on you ensure that you only place it on soft surfaces.
Ensure that the jewellery box is always stored in a clean and dry place.
Caring for
Diamonds and Gemstones
Avoid testing your diamond by scratching it on a glass surface or a beer bottle, contrary to the common myth it might actually damage your diamonds.
Avoid extreme and constant exposure to heat and light as some gemstones might break under extreme heat or loose colour under prolonged exposure to sunlight.
If your jewellery takes a hard hit or is subjected to unnatural motion, it is advisable you get it checked to ensure that the settings are not loose, this will prevent you from losing your diamonds or gemstones.
Cleaning
your Jewellery
Use a solution of luke warm water with mild dish soap (do not use liquid or solid detergents under any cost) and soft makeup brush to clean your jewellery.
Never rinse the jewellery directly in a sink as the water pressure may loosen the stones, take a glass of water and rinse the jewellery in it
Organic gems like pearls can easily be scratched so always use a clean makeup brush and warm soapy solution to clean them and let them dry thoroughly before using them again.
Use only jewellery polishing cloth to wipe the jewellery, never use a tissue or any kind of paper as the fibres present in them can lead to scratches on the jewellery.
